Fraser is the brand of ultra-luxury yachts (over 100 meters long), in full expansion, which wants to totally redefine its digital ecosystem in order to offer a seamless phygital experience. I made this work at my previous agency, Emakina.

I help them to

• Sort & identify main brand opportunities
• Challenge majors client experience axes
• Build the future brand digital client experience
• Create a clear roadmap to achieve it

Challenge

Helping the brand with the world’s most sophisticaded clients, to become the reference in the market by creating the best and the most digital experience in the world

Insight

Research & interviews revealed that the only way to provide this type of experience is to be aside of the client during all the steps of experience. The secret : Furnish a custom experience for each client with a privilegied contact for everything.

So how to frame a full tailored digital experience at a new level of ultra-luxury for exclusive people?

Work, Implication, Role

Within the Emakina FR team and in collaboration with Emakina CEE, we first conducted a series of interviews that allowed us to identify the desires, needs and frustrations of Fraser's clients. What were their favorite means of communication and what experience did they expect, so that we could surprise them.

Following this first step, with Fraser's teams, we conducted several weeks of design workshops using design methods such as "How might we" or "Crazy Heights" to challenge the experience. Major experience axes were identified and allowed us to create a true global overview of the entire customer experience and to propose a roadmap to implement it.

Results

2 Months of Design Workshops, 12 hours per week we:

• Establish 5 Personas
• 8 customer journeys for each personas
• Define +50 experience keys
• Craft & Implement them in webdesign
• Build the web experience tree

Deliverables

• + 50 ultra-luxury experience solutions deck
• Experience Actionable Roadmap
• Web Experience tree
• Webdesign Experience prototypes
• Complete Workshop reports
